Guest feedback analytics that operators actually read

Most guest feedback analytics tools produce dashboards nobody opens. ProInsights produces a morning email and a WhatsApp message your GM reads with coffee — and a dashboard that backs it up when you want to dig deeper. Built for hotels and restaurants that want signal, not analytics theatre.

THE PROBLEM WITH DASHBOARDS

You did not buy a hotel to learn a BI tool.

The classic problem with guest feedback analytics is that the answer to "what did our guests say last week?" is buried three clicks deep in a dashboard nobody on the operations side has time to open. So nobody opens it. So nothing changes.

ProInsights inverts the model. The intelligence comes to you — in your inbox, on your phone — in a format you can read in two minutes. The dashboard is the archive for when you need to dig, not the daily workflow.

WHAT WE ANALYSE

Every guest review, read by AI, sorted by what it tells you.

Sentiment

Every review scored as positive, mixed or negative — not just by overall stars but by theme. A review that praises the room and criticises breakfast registers both signals correctly.

Themes

Cleanliness, service, value, location, breakfast, noise, wait time, pace — auto-detected, ranked by frequency and movement over time.

Named entities

Staff members, dishes, room numbers, amenities — extracted by name and counted, so the language of your team and guests becomes structured data.

DAILY, WEEKLY, MONTHLY

Three cadences, each tuned to its audience.

Daily report. What changed overnight, what to act on today. Aimed at the GM, the duty manager, the F&B head — the people running the floor.

Weekly report. Trend lines per theme, competitor side-by-side, top praised and top criticised items of the week. Aimed at the DOM, the F&B director, the head of housekeeping.

Monthly management report. A board-ready summary with rating movement, sentiment movement per department, and a written commentary. Aimed at ownership and the executive committee.

DASHBOARD AND EXPORT

A dashboard that supports the email, not the other way around.

The web dashboard archives every report you have ever received and lets you slice the underlying data: by source, by date range, by theme, by staff member, by competitor. Export to PDF for board packs. Filter by review type for a performance review. Pull thirty days of breakfast sentiment for the F&B director.

But none of this is required to get value from the product. Most users open the dashboard once a fortnight. The email and WhatsApp do the day-to-day work.

SOURCES

The seven sources we read, and only those seven.

For hotels: Google, TripAdvisor, Booking.com, Expedia, Trip.com, ZenHotels and HolidayCheck. For restaurants: Google and TripAdvisor. That is the full list. If a source matters to you and is not in that list, ask — we will tell you honestly whether we can add it or not. We do not pretend to monitor sources we cannot reliably read.

Frequently asked

Questions, answered.

What is the difference between ProInsights and a generic NPS or survey tool?
NPS and survey tools measure feedback you actively collect. ProInsights measures feedback your guests are already leaving in public — Google, TripAdvisor, the OTAs. They are complementary; we do not replace your post-stay survey.
Can the report be customised per recipient?
The body of the report is the same for everyone on the recipient list, so the team is aligned around one version of the truth. The WhatsApp summary on Pro is a condensed version of the same brief.
How is sentiment measured?
Every review is read by Claude AI and scored on overall sentiment plus sentiment per theme. The model is updated as the underlying language model evolves, so accuracy improves over time.
Can I see historical trend lines?
Yes. Weekly and monthly reports show trend lines by theme. The dashboard allows custom date-range queries down to the day for any theme or staff member you have tracked.
Do you support hotels and restaurants on the same account?
Yes. A group with multiple properties — including a mix of hotels and restaurants — gets each property on the right vertical, with multi-property discount on second and subsequent properties.
