01

What's in every issue.

Each daily briefing is shaped by the same six elements. None of them are dashboards. All of them fit in a phone screen.

An AI Executive Brief at the top.

Three sentences telling you what changed since yesterday. What's improving, what's slipping, the one thing to act on first. Generated fresh by Claude every morning from the actual reviews and the actual movements in the data.

The new reviews, sorted by what matters.

Any new 5-star with names a staff member by name. Any new 1- or 2-star with a pre-drafted diplomatic reply for your team to edit and publish. Sensitive content flagged for manual handling rather than auto-replied.

Your competitors, ranked by movement.

Five to ten named competitors, snapshotted daily. We surface the ones that moved more than ±0.1 star, plus a weekly strengths-and-weaknesses pull-out so you know exactly what they are being praised and criticised for.

Sub-scores for hotels, sommeliers for restaurants.

Hotels get Booking and Google Hotels sub-scores broken out — cleanliness, comfort, location, value. Restaurants get dish leaderboards and named-staff mentions. The intelligence adapts to your vertical, not the other way around.

A WhatsApp companion at the same moment.

The five lines that matter, on your phone. Star delta vs yesterday. Urgent items. Best review. Competitor on the move. Under 1000 characters. Pro and Expert tiers.

A 30-minute alert when a 1-star lands.

During your operating hours, any new 1-star at any tracked venue sends a WhatsApp and an email within half an hour. Includes the text, sentiment context, and a draft reply. Outside operating hours, alerts queue for the next morning.

02

How an issue is made.

You name your venues — the Google Business Profile, the TripAdvisor page, the Booking listing if it's a hotel — and five to ten competitors that genuinely keep you up at night. That's a fifteen-minute call.

From the next morning onward, the system pulls new reviews across all configured sources every day at dawn. They get translated if they aren't already in your operating language. They get analysed for sentiment, themes, named dishes, named staff. They get cross-referenced against the last thirty days so we can tell what's a one-off and what's a pattern.

Claude writes the brief — three sentences, no fluff. The renderer assembles the email and the WhatsApp message. By eight in the morning, local time, the issue is in your inbox and on your phone. You read it over coffee. You brief the team in pre-shift. You move on with the day.

If a one-star comes in at lunch, the alert pipeline fires within half an hour during operating hours. If a competitor drops two notches overnight, you find out in tomorrow's issue.

There is nothing to log into. There is nothing to maintain. There is no software on your phone. You read the email; that is the workflow.

03

Why this exists.

The reputation-management category for hospitality is mature. Revinate, ReviewPro, Trustyou, Customer Alliance — all of them work. All of them are good at what they do. But all of them were built for the enterprise hotel chain: heavy onboarding, an annual contract, a dashboard that requires a dedicated revenue manager to interpret. They start at €500 a month and they go up from there.

That is the wrong product for the operator we built ProInsights for. The owner of three restaurants in a Cypriot coastal town. The general manager of a fifty-room family hotel in the Peloponnese. The director of a single-property Michelin-listed restaurant in Düsseldorf. None of these people want a dashboard. They want a quiet, intelligent assistant who reads everything the internet wrote about them overnight, distils it into something useful, and goes away.

So we built that. The email is the product. The intelligence is the moat. The price reflects who it's for, not what the enterprise market will bear.
