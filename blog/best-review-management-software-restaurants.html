Best review management software for restaurants in 2026

Most "best of" lists for restaurant review software are sponsored placements dressed as guides. This one tries to be useful instead. We have grouped seven tools by the use case each one actually fits, with honest notes on what they do well, where they fall short, and what they cost when you ask for a real quote.

If you take nothing else from this article: there is no single best tool. There is a best tool for a single 40-cover bistro in Limassol, and a different best tool for a 14-site casual-dining group in Athens, and a different one again for a four-location group that just opened its first international branch.

Independent single-location restaurants

If you run one site, the honest first question is whether you need software at all. A disciplined manager with a Google Alert, the TripAdvisor management center and a Google Business Profile login can do a perfectly good job for free. The software cost only starts to pay back when one of three things is true:

  • You are missing reviews because they land on platforms you do not check daily.
  • You want a draft reply waiting for you each morning rather than starting from scratch.
  • You want one daily message that summarises everything, so you can decide in two minutes whether to dig deeper.

What we would actually recommend at this stage:

  1. The free native combo if you have the discipline to check three back ends every morning.
  2. ProInsights if you want one daily WhatsApp/email digest covering Google, TripAdvisor and Booking.com, plus draft replies. It is built for this size of business and is one of the cheaper paid options in the category.
  3. MARA if you specifically want help drafting replies and you are happy to keep monitoring in your existing routine. It is narrower in scope than ProInsights, but excellent at the one thing it does.

Small chains (2-10 locations)

This is the size at which the spreadsheet always breaks. You start with one manager handling reviews across three sites, you grow to five, someone goes on holiday, two reviews are missed, an owner sees them, and the conversation about software starts.

What matters most at this size:

  • Per-location filtering, so each GM gets only their own reviews.
  • A consolidated weekly view for the owner or operations director.
  • Competitor benchmarking — once you have multiple sites, you also have multiple competitive sets.

Realistic options:

  1. ProInsights for groups headquartered in Europe or the Middle East. Multi-location pricing, GM-level digests, and competitor benchmarking are included from the lower tiers.
  2. ReviewTrackers if you are US-centric or already integrated with Yelp at scale. Strong on the analytics side, weaker on European platforms like Booking.com.
  3. Birdeye if you want messaging, surveys and listings management in the same tool, and you do not mind a higher monthly cost.

Multi-location groups (10+ sites, mixed brands)

At this size, review management becomes part of a broader "customer experience" stack. You usually have a marketing team, a brand standards function, and a CFO who wants ROI on every line item. Three things move to the front of the requirements list:

  1. Brand-level reporting. CMO wants to see Brand A vs Brand B across all sites.
  2. Role-based access. GMs see their site only. Regional managers see their cluster. The CFO sees everything.
  3. Integrations. POS, CRM, survey platform, listings management.

At this stage, the realistic shortlist narrows to Reputation.com, Birdeye and Yext Reviews. Each is expensive and each requires an implementation project (3-6 months) rather than a sign-up. The trade-off is that they fit cleanly into a wider marketing stack.

A note: groups in this category sometimes deploy a lighter tool like ProInsights or ReviewTrackers in parallel for the operational layer, so that GMs and area managers get a daily working tool while marketing uses the enterprise platform for reporting. This is a sensible split.

Tool-by-tool honest notes

ProInsights

Strengths. Daily WhatsApp and email digests are the differentiator — most competitors only do email. Cheap entry tier. Cyprus / Greece / Mediterranean focus shows up in the way Greek and Greeklish reviews are handled. Draft replies are short and on-brand. Competitor benchmarking is included.

Weaknesses. Smaller team than the US incumbents, so the integration list is shorter. No native survey tool yet. Not a fit for very large enterprise rollouts (hundreds of locations).

ReviewTrackers

Strengths. Mature, US-market leader. Strong Yelp integration. Solid analytics, good API for groups that want to pipe review data into a data warehouse.

Weaknesses. Pricing is higher than European alternatives. Booking.com and TripAdvisor coverage exists but is not their strongest area.

Birdeye

Strengths. Broad platform — reviews, messaging, surveys, listings, payments. Useful if you want one vendor for all customer-facing touchpoints.

Weaknesses. Expensive. Aggressive sales process. The breadth is a double-edged sword: if you only need reviews, you are paying for nine other modules.

Yext Reviews

Strengths. Tight integration with Yext's listings management. Strong if you are already running locations through Yext.

Weaknesses. Not really a standalone product. The reviews module is thinner than dedicated competitors.

Reputation.com

Strengths. Enterprise depth. Survey tools, CX analytics, board-level reporting. Used by some of the largest hotel and restaurant chains.

Weaknesses. Cost. Complexity. Not appropriate below 20-30 locations.

MARA

Strengths. Specifically built for reply drafting. The drafts are well-written. Cheap.

Weaknesses. Narrow scope — does not aggregate or alert. You still need a way to know that a review has arrived.

Native Google + TripAdvisor + Booking.com back ends

Strengths. Free. Direct from source. No data middleman.

Weaknesses. Three logins, three different UIs, no consolidated view, email notifications that often miss. Breaks the moment your manager is on holiday.

There is no single best tool. There is a best tool for one 40-cover bistro, and a different one for a 14-site group.

How to actually choose

Three short questions decide most of this:

  1. How many locations do you have, and how many will you have in 18 months? Below five, stay with a lightweight tool. Above twenty, talk to enterprise vendors.
  2. Who is the daily user? If it is a GM with two minutes before service, you need a digest, not a dashboard. If it is a marketing analyst, you need a dashboard, not a digest.
  3. What is the smallest paid plan and does it have a real trial? If a vendor will not let you trial the product for 14 days at minimum, that is information.

Bottom line

For an independent restaurant or a small group based in Europe or the Mediterranean, the realistic 2026 shortlist is: free native tools, ProInsights, or MARA. For larger groups, add ReviewTrackers and Birdeye. For enterprise, the conversation is Reputation.com against Birdeye against Yext. Run a paid pilot before you commit to any of them for more than three months.

Frequently asked

Questions, answered.

Do I need review management software if I already use Google Business Profile?
Not for one location with a disciplined manager. The case starts to make sense at two or three locations, or when you also want to monitor TripAdvisor and Booking.com without logging into each.
Will software remove fake reviews automatically?
No. Any tool that claims to remove reviews automatically is misleading you. Software can help you spot likely fakes and prepare evidence; only the platforms themselves remove reviews, and only when their guidelines are breached.
How long does implementation take?
From under an hour for a single-location signup with ProInsights or MARA, to 3-6 months for an enterprise rollout with Reputation.com or Yext.
Can review software draft replies for me?
Most modern tools include AI-drafted replies. Quality varies. The realistic test is to read 20 sample drafts before you sign — if you'd send 16 of them with minor edits, the tool is good enough.
What's the cheapest credible option for a single restaurant?
Free native back ends if you have the discipline to check them daily. If you want everything in one daily message with draft replies, ProInsights and MARA both have entry tiers under €50 a month.
